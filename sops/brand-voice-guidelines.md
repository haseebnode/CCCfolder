# SOP: Brand Voice Guidelines

## Purpose
Maintain consistent brand voice across all copywriting to build recognition, trust, and connection with the audience.

---

## Our Brand Voice

### Core Attributes

**1. Conversational, Not Corporate**
- ✅ Write like you talk
- ✅ Use contractions (we're, don't, can't)
- ✅ Ask questions
- ❌ Avoid jargon and buzzwords
- ❌ No corporate speak ("synergies", "leverage", "paradigm shift")

**Example**:
- ❌ "We leverage cutting-edge technology to optimize your workflow paradigm"
- ✅ "We help you get more done without the headaches"

---

**2. Confident, Not Arrogant**
- ✅ State facts clearly
- ✅ Show proof with data
- ✅ Acknowledge customer's intelligence
- ❌ Don't overpromise
- ❌ Don't talk down to audience
- ❌ Avoid superlatives without proof ("best", "ultimate", "revolutionary")

**Example**:
- ❌ "We're the #1 revolutionary platform that will completely transform your business"
- ✅ "We helped 200+ companies reduce churn by an average of 40%"

---

**3. Helpful, Not Pushy**
- ✅ Lead with value
- ✅ Educate first, sell second
- ✅ Respect their decision
- ❌ No aggressive sales tactics
- ❌ No artificial urgency ("Act now!", "Last chance!")
- ❌ No guilt trips

**Example**:
- ❌ "This offer expires in 24 hours - don't miss out or you'll regret it!"
- ✅ "Start your free trial today - no credit card needed, cancel anytime"

---

**4. Clear, Not Clever**
- ✅ Get to the point quickly
- ✅ Use simple words
- ✅ Short sentences and paragraphs
- ❌ No puns or wordplay (unless genuinely funny)
- ❌ No obscure references
- ❌ No flowery language

**Example**:
- ❌ "Embark on a transformative journey to operational excellence"
- ✅ "Ship projects on time, every time"

---

**5. Human, Not Robotic**
- ✅ Show personality
- ✅ Use occasional humor (when appropriate)
- ✅ Admit mistakes
- ❌ No stiff, formal language
- ❌ No passive voice (when possible)
- ❌ No hiding behind corporate identity

**Example**:
- ❌ "An error has been detected in the system"
- ✅ "We made a mistake and we're fixing it"

## Writing Guidelines by Format

### Email Copy

**Subject Lines**:
- 40-50 characters max
- Personalization when possible: "{{first_name}}, quick question"
- Curiosity gap, not clickbait
- A/B test everything

**Example**:
- ❌ "URGENT: Limited Time Offer Inside!!!"
- ✅ "Quick question about {{company_name}}'s customer retention"

**Body Copy**:
- First sentence = hook (pattern interrupt, question, bold statement)
- Keep paragraphs to 1-2 sentences
- One clear CTA per email
- Use bullet points for scannability
- Sign off like a human: "Best," "Thanks," "Cheers,"

**Example**:
```
Hi {{first_name}},

I noticed {{company_name}} just launched a new product line - congrats!

Quick question: how are you handling customer onboarding at scale?

Most companies I talk to struggle to keep new users engaged past the first week. We built a tool that automates the first 30 days - users who complete it are 3x more likely to convert to paid.

Worth a quick chat?

Best,
[Name]
```

---

### Landing Pages

**Headlines**:
- Lead with benefit, not feature
- Speak to specific pain point
- Include target audience (if relevant)
- Under 10 words when possible

**Example**:
- ❌ "All-in-One Project Management Platform"
- ✅ "Never Miss Another Deadline"

**Subheadlines**:
- Expand on the promise
- Include mechanism or proof
- 1-2 sentences max

**Example**:
- ✅ "Turn chaos into clarity with one source of truth for your entire team. Used by 2,000+ marketing teams."

**Body Copy**:
- Problem → Solution → Proof → CTA
- Use headers for scannability
- Benefits > Features
- Show, don't tell (use specific examples)

**CTAs**:
- Action-oriented
- Specific (not just "Submit" or "Sign Up")
- Remove friction
- Include risk reversal

**Example**:
- ❌ "Submit"
- ✅ "Start Free 14-Day Trial" (No credit card required)

---

### Social Media

**LinkedIn**:
- Professional but conversational
- Lead with insight or question
- Tag relevant people/companies
- Include call to action (comment, share, visit link)

**Twitter/X**:
- Punchy and direct
- One clear idea per tweet
- Use threads for longer thoughts
- Hashtags sparingly (1-2 max)

**Example**:
- ✅ "Most SaaS companies lose 60% of new users in the first week. The reason? Onboarding is treated as a nice-to-have, not a core product feature. What's your first-week activation rate?"

---

### Ad Copy

**Headline**:
- Benefit-driven
- Speak to pain point or desire
- Specific, not generic

**Description**:
- Expand on benefit
- Include social proof if space allows
- Clear CTA

**Example**:
- Headline: "Reduce Customer Churn by 40%"
- Description: "Automated feedback tool used by 200+ SaaS companies. See why customers leave before it's too late. Start free trial."

## Words to Use

### Power Words (Use These)
- You, Your (reader-focused)
- Free
- Because (reason-why)
- New
- Now
- Discover
- Proven
- Results
- Easy
- Guaranteed (if you actually guarantee it)

### Transition Words (For Flow)
- Here's why
- For example
- In fact
- The truth is
- Here's the thing
- Bottom line

### Social Proof Indicators
- Join [X] companies
- Trusted by [X] teams
- Used by [specific notable customer]
- Rated [X] stars by [X] users

---

## Words to Avoid

### Jargon & Buzzwords
- ❌ Synergy
- ❌ Leverage (as a verb)
- ❌ Paradigm shift
- ❌ Disruptive (unless literally disrupting)
- ❌ Game-changer (overused)
- ❌ Next-generation
- ❌ Cutting-edge
- ❌ Revolutionary (without proof)
- ❌ World-class
- ❌ Best-in-breed
- ❌ Turnkey solution
- ❌ Low-hanging fruit

### Spam Trigger Words
- ❌ Free (in subject line)
- ❌ Urgent
- ❌ Act now
- ❌ Limited time
- ❌ Guarantee (in subject line)
- ❌ Risk-free (in subject line)
- ❌ Click here
- ❌ All caps (LIKE THIS)
- ❌ Excessive punctuation (!!!)

## Formatting Standards

### General
- Use sentence case for headlines (not Title Case)
- Use bold sparingly (key points only)
- Use italics for emphasis, not bold + italics
- Bullet points for lists, not numbered (unless order matters)
- Emojis: OK in email (sparingly), avoid in formal content

### Numbers
- Spell out one through nine
- Use numerals for 10+
- Use commas for thousands (10,000 not 10000)
- Round big numbers (2.3M not 2,347,982)
- Use % symbol, not "percent"

### Links
- Use descriptive link text, not "click here"
- ❌ "Click here to learn more"
- ✅ "See how Company X reduced churn by 40%"

## Before Publishing Checklist

- [ ] Does it sound conversational? (Read it out loud)
- [ ] Is it benefit-focused? (WIIFM - What's In It For Me?)
- [ ] Is it specific? (Avoid vague claims)
- [ ] Is it clear? (Would a 12-year-old understand?)
- [ ] Is the CTA obvious? (What do you want them to do?)
- [ ] Does it match our voice? (Confident, helpful, human)
- [ ] No jargon or buzzwords?
- [ ] No spam trigger words?
- [ ] Proofread for typos?
- [ ] Mobile-friendly? (Short paragraphs, scannable)

## Examples: Before & After

### Example 1: Email

**Before**:
```
Subject: Exciting Opportunity to Revolutionize Your Workflow

Dear Sir/Madam,

We would like to take this opportunity to introduce our cutting-edge platform that leverages AI-powered technology to optimize your team's productivity paradigm. Our best-in-breed solution offers synergistic benefits that will transform the way you work.

Please click here to schedule a consultation.

Regards,
Sales Team
```

**After**:
```
Subject: Quick question about {{company_name}}'s project deadlines

Hi {{first_name}},

Do you ever feel like your team is drowning in Slack messages and lost spreadsheets?

We built a tool that helps marketing teams like yours ship campaigns on time - without the chaos. One source of truth for all your projects, deadlines, and deliverables.

{{similar_company}} went from missing 30% of deadlines to 100% on-time delivery in 60 days.

Worth a quick call to see if it's a fit?

Best,
Sarah
```
